Scheme of Amalgamation

The Scheme of Amalgamation shall contain all the material facts about the company, its financial situation, the Auditor’s report, pendency of any proceeding or investigation against the company etc. Information regarding the reduction in the share capital of the company.

 

Scheme of Corporate debt restructuring

Such scheme should be approved by 75% of the corporate debtors including:

a. The Creditors responsibility statement under Form No.CAA 1,

b. Safeguards for the other secured/unsecured creditors

c. The auditor’s report regarding the liquidity test of the company on the basis of the data provided by the board.

d. The statement regarding the conformation of the guidelines issued by RBI pursuant to corporate debt restructuring.

e. The valuation report of the assets of the company, tangible and non-tangible.

 

1.     The tribunal may order meeting between the creditors and members of the company.

2.     Notice of the meeting shall be sent to the person/class of person such as creditor, member, debenture holders under form CAA-2.

3.     The notice of amalgamation scheme or compromise or arrangement shall be sent to Central government, RBI, CCI, ROC, Official Liquidator, or any other sectoral liquidator or are supposed to be affected by the amalgamation or demerger, under form CAA 3.

4.     A meeting of members, creditors should approve the scheme by majority.

5.     Where the tribunal sanctions the scheme of amalgamation or demerger, the order shall include such direction in reference to any modification which is required and proper for the working of the amalgamation.

6.     The amalgamation shall take place under form CAA 6.
